Why Not Democratic Socialism?

Democratic Techno-Socio-Capitalism maintains free markets, competition, and private enterprise. The distinction matters:

Markets remain competitive: Companies compete, innovate, and operate independently. No state ownership of production. Antitrust enforcement prevents monopolies, but private business continues.

Redistribution, not revolution: Wealth and automation taxes fund universal services (healthcare, UBI) without eliminating capitalism. Profit motive exists; gains are simply shared more broadly.

Technology-focused: Specifically designed for automated economies where traditional labor markets fail. Not ideological opposition to markets—pragmatic response to technological displacement.

Surveillance with safeguards: Accepts that data collection exists; structures it with legal firewalls and zero-knowledge verification rather than attempting to eliminate it entirely.

This framework preserves what works about capitalism (innovation, efficiency, choice) while addressing what fails (wealth concentration, automation displacement, surveillance abuse). It's techno-socio-capitalism: market economy with strong social protection and technological adaptation.
